Project 2: Visual Analytics — Amazon Product Engagement

Dataset: Amazon Products Sales (≈42k items, 2025)

Problem

What actually drives product engagement on Amazon? I focused on four things: (1) how reviews are distributed, (2) whether ratings separate winners from the pack, (3) which categories punch above their weight, and (4) how discounting relates to reviews and ratings.

Data

Columns used: discounted_price, original_price, discount_percentage, product_rating, total_reviews, product_category. ~42,000 products scraped from Amazon. Not official; good enough for exploratory visual analytics.

Prep

  • Forced numeric types for price, discount, rating, and review count; dropped rows missing critical fields.
  • Handled heavy skew in total_reviews with log-scale visuals and outlier clipping where needed.
  • Binned discounts into discount_band: No Discount, 0–20%, 20–50%, 50%+.

Visualizations

Takeaways

  • Engagement is brutal and unequal. The median product is quiet; a tiny fraction soaks up attention.
  • Ratings are compressed. They won’t rank-order winners. Review volume is the signal.
  • Category matters for visibility (Phones, Power/Batteries) more than for ratings.
  • Mid-range discounts (20–50%) correlate with more reviews; fire-sale 50%+ doesn’t help quality or volume much.

Limits

  • Scraped snapshot, not official; no time dimension to prove causality.
  • Review count is a proxy for engagement, not revenue or units sold.
  • Category taxonomy may be noisy; some items cross categories.

Repro

Notebook loads the CSV, coerces numerics, bins discounts, and produces the charts above. Figures are exported to figs/ and referenced here.
