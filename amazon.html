Project 2: Visual Analytics — Amazon Product Engagement

Dataset: ikramshah512 — Amazon Products Sales (≈42k items, 2025).

Problem

Explore drivers of product engagement on Amazon: (1) how reviews are distributed, (2) whether ratings separate winners from average, (3) which categories dominate, and (4) how discounting affects reviews and satisfaction.

Data

CSV with columns: discounted_price, original_price, discount_percentage, product_rating, total_reviews, product_category. About 42k scraped products. Unofficial dataset, but solid for practice.

Pre-processing

  • Converted numeric fields to floats/ints; dropped rows missing key fields.
  • Clipped extreme review outliers; used log scale to show skewed distribution.
  • Binned discounts into discount_band: No Discount, 0–20%, 20–50%, 50%+.

Visualizations

What I Learned

  • Engagement is highly unequal — most items are quiet, a few dominate reviews.
  • Ratings are compressed and don’t separate winners; volume does.
  • Category visibility matters more than rating quality.
  • Mid-range discounts (20–50%) align with higher reviews; fire-sale 50%+ doesn’t help much.

Impact & Limits

This analysis shows how reviews and discounts interact with product visibility. It’s based on a snapshot of scraped data — no time element, no sales/revenue confirmation. Reviews are a proxy for engagement, not direct revenue or units sold.

References

  • Kaggle dataset: Amazon Products Sales (42k items, 2025)
  • Libraries: pandas, matplotlib, seaborn
