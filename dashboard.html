Project 2: Interactive Dashboard – Amazon Product Engagement

Dataset: ikramshah512 — Amazon Products Sales (≈42k items, 2025)

Overview

This project transforms the static analysis from Project 1 into a fully interactive Streamlit dashboard that allows users to explore product engagement drivers on Amazon. The dashboard connects ratings, discounts, coupons, and category visibility to engagement (measured by total_reviews).

Audience & Purpose

Audience: Amazon merchandising and category managers.
Purpose: Provide an interactive tool to evaluate how discount strategy, coupons, and sponsorships influence visibility and customer engagement across product categories.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Average Rating – proxy for customer satisfaction.
  • Total Reviews – measures product engagement and visibility.
  • Average Discount % – reflects promotion strategy and pricing leverage.
  • Best-Seller Share % – percent of top-ranked products within filtered view.

These KPIs balance what the audience can control (pricing, promotions) and what they want to understand (engagement and satisfaction).

Dashboard Structure & Interactivity

  • KPI Row: Four summary metrics update instantly when filters change.
  • Filters: Category, rating range, price range, coupon flag, sponsorship flag.
  • Charts:
    • Histogram of product ratings – shows compression around 4–5 stars.
    • Scatterplot of purchases vs rating – color-coded by sponsorship for easy comparison.
  • Predictor Tool: A regression model (trained in train_model.py) estimates expected review count for a hypothetical product using price, rating, discount, coupon, and category inputs.
  • Diagnostics: Displays R², MAE, and log–log Actual vs Predicted plot for model reliability.

Design Choices

  • Platform: Streamlit cloud for quick, interactive deployment and easy public sharing.
  • Layout: Vertical flow (KPIs → Charts → Predictor → Diagnostics) for natural reading.
  • Visuals: Simple, uncluttered, readable color scheme with Plotly defaults.
  • Function over flash: Didnt really do anything too colorful, I wanted my actual model and plots to be interpreted well.

Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

  • Converted all numeric fields (prices, ratings, reviews) to proper numeric types.
  • Dropped rows missing key data (rating, reviews, price).
  • Created discount_percent, price_delta, and price_ratio for analysis and model input.
  • Normalized categorical flags (has_coupon, is_sponsored) to 0 / 1 values.
  • Capped extreme outliers to reduce distortion in histograms and model fit.

Reflection

  • What worked: Filters and KPIs made it easy to connect pricing levers to engagement metrics, the model works but could be improved, happy to have it on there though.
  • Challenges: Cleaning coupon/sponsorship text data and handling extreme skew in review counts as well as actually hosting the dashboard.
  • Could Improve: Definetly making the visuals look a lot better, feel like they were lacking for this project.
