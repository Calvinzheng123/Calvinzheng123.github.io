Project 1: Weather Data Exploration

Dataset: prasad22 — Weather Data (synthetic).

⚠️ One or more charts failed to load. Check /images/ filenames.

Problem

Choose among a few U.S. cities for outdoor plans with comfortable temperatures and lower rain risk. Using synthetic data, I (1) show temperature patterns over time, (2) summarize monthly rainfall by city, and (3) check how temperature aligns with humidity (comfort proxy). Goal: identify months to target vs. avoid.

Who cares: travelers, event planners, and anyone scheduling outdoor activities.

Data

CSV columns: Location, Date_Time, Temperature_F, Humidity_pct, Precipitation_mm, Wind_Speed_kmh. This is synthetic training data, which is good for methods practice, not for real-world climate decisions.

Methods

  • Parsed Date_Time to datetime; derived daily and monthly groupings. Why: daily captures seasonality detail; monthly aligns with planning windows.
  • Validated shape/columns; dropped rows missing Date_Time or Location (none critical). Why: ensures clean timeline and city IDs before aggregation.
  • Checked duplicates on (Location, Date_Time); none affecting aggregates. Why: prevents double-counting observations.
  • Aggregations:
    • Daily temperature: mean °F by city/date. Why: shows seasonal cycles at the right granularity.
    • Monthly precipitation: total mm by city/month. Why: monthly totals matter for travel/events planning.
    • Temp–humidity hexbin: density plot. Why: avoids scatter overplotting; highlights common “feel-like” zones.

Visualizations

Findings

  • Seasonality: Cities show predictable warm/cool phases. Plan outdoors just before peak heat; shoulder periods are most comfortable.
  • Rain risk: Monthly totals flag wetter windows. If you’re date-constrained, pick the city with both lower median and tighter spread in your target month.
  • Comfort proxy: Hexbin clusters around moderate temp + moderate humidity indicate the best “feel-like” zone for activities.

If this were real data: shortlist months with stable temps (gentler slope in daily lines), avoid city month pairs with the fattest precip bars, and sanity-check that your pick sits near the hexbin’s moderate cluster.

So What?

  • Vacationers: Target shoulder months for comfort; keep an indoor backup if monthly precip variance is high.
  • Event planners: Prefer cities with low rain totals and low variance in your window; book tents/coverage otherwise.
  • Outdoor teams/leagues: Schedule where the hexbin center aligns with target practice conditions.

Impact & Limits

This is a methods demo on synthetic data with no real-world inference. Misusing it for actual planning could mislead decisions. Missing features (pressure, cloud cover, heat index) limit comfort modeling; short time span may hide rare extremes.
